TinyOpenJam

what is a tinyopenjam?

It's a plan of attack for accomplishing a project. The goal of a Tiny Open Jam is to make a sizeable amount of progress without feeling too overwhelmed to start it.

Tiny

Think of the smallest amount of value you'd be satisfied adding to your project before proudly showing it off to someone.

Go from Complete to Complete -- don't leave your project in an unusable state or less "finished" than when you started!

Open

Complete your work with the expectation that others will view it upon completion.

In the extreme, this could mean the work you do is open-source (e.g. code). In fact, this is encouraged.

Jam

The work can be completed in the uninterrupted period of time you set aside.

Other definitions: improvising in order to accomplish your work, can be completed with friends, can involve rocking out to music.

How can I start my own tinyopenjam?

It's pretty simple. All you need is a goal and a deadline.

Make sure your goal is tiny. If it seems too daunting to accomplish, make it tinier. But also make it significant enough so that when you complete it, the first thing you want to do is tweet about it, insta-share it, and tell all your friends.

For your deadline, make sure it's soon enough that you can jam without being interrupted. You can always take breaks (and we encourage it), but during this time you should primarily be focused on getting your work done.

The rest of TinyOpenJam is just an attitude thing. Keep the three principles in mind while you work and you'll be good to go!
